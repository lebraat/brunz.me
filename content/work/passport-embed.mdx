## Context

Human Passport had established itself as a privacy-preserving identity verification platform — but it was a standalone product. Partners who wanted to verify users had to send them to Passport's interface, breaking their own UX flow. The embed changed that: a drop-in component that any application could integrate directly.

## What I did

**Defined the product requirements and use cases** with the engineering team. The key insight was that embed users have fundamentally different needs than standalone Passport users — they need fewer options, faster verification, and seamless visual integration with the host application.

**Led GTM planning** including positioning, launch messaging, and developer documentation. The positioning shift was significant: from "Passport is for DAOs" to "Passport is for any application that needs identity."

**Coordinated the partner launch** with early adopters, handling integration support and feedback loops to refine the developer experience before wider release.

## Platform context

The embed launched as part of a broader growth period for Human Passport:

- Scaled from **6 to 126 campaigns** (2,000% increase)
- Grew user base to **1.8M+ users** (450% growth)
- Platform protecting **$430M+** in value
- **25+ stamp providers** integrated
- Generated **$387K revenue** in 6 months

## Takeaway

Taking a core capability and wrapping it in developer-friendly packaging is a specific product skill. The embed didn't change what Passport could do — it changed who could use it and where. That distribution unlock was the real product.
